# Punt Article Writing Guide

Use this guide when drafting articles in Claude Pro (or writing manually). Paste the final draft into the Punt admin editor.

## Positioning

Punt explains Irish financial events plainly. It is educational, not advisory. It is a signpost to official sources and MABS, not a news publisher.

## Strict rules

Every article must:
- Be factual and sourced. Every claim should be traceable to a named publication (RTÉ, Irish Times, Independent, Examiner, Business Post) or official source (gov.ie, centralbank.ie, ccpc.ie).
- Avoid advice. Never use "you should", "we recommend", "the best option", "the right choice", "it would be wise to", or any directive suggesting a specific financial action.
- Avoid predictions. Never say "rates are likely to rise", "this will probably mean", "experts expect". Report what is, not what might be.
- Avoid editorial framing. Never say "this is bad news for customers", "this benefits shareholders at the expense of customers", or any evaluative judgement.
- Avoid defamation. Never accuse any named company of wrongdoing or mismanagement. If a source makes such a claim, attribute it clearly and reproduce their wording.

## Structure

1. **What happened** (1-2 paragraphs, factual, sourced)
2. **How this works, plainly** (2-4 paragraphs, mechanical explanation — e.g., when a bank sells mortgages, what legally happens to the contract, the terms, the rate, the direct debit)
3. **What doesn't change** (reassurance paragraph if applicable — e.g., consumer rights preserved by law)
4. **Where to go for more** (link to related Punt lessons, MABS, CCPC)

Length: 400-700 words. Reading time: 3-5 minutes.

## Tone

Warm, plain, second-person. Short sentences. Active voice. Explain terms when used.

## Acceptable example (about a bank sale)

> PTSB has been sold to BAWAG, an Austrian banking group. Under Irish consumer credit law, the terms of your existing mortgage — your interest rate, your remaining term, and your monthly repayment amount — cannot be changed by this sale. The name on your statements and your direct debit may change over the coming months, but your contract stays the same.

## Unacceptable example (same topic)

> PTSB's sale to BAWAG is worrying for customers, who should consider switching banks before rates rise. Experts say BAWAG is likely to increase charges...

Every sentence here violates a rule: editorialising, advising, predicting, vague sourcing.

## Prompt to use in Claude Pro when drafting

When drafting in Claude Pro, start the conversation with something like:

> I'm drafting an article for Punt, an Irish financial literacy platform. Please read these writing guidelines [paste this file], then help me write an educational explainer on [topic] based on these sources: [paste URLs/text]. Remember: educational, not advisory. No "you should" language. Factual and sourced.

Then paste the final approved version into the Punt admin editor.

## Disclaimer

The disclaimer ("Educational content only. Not financial advice...") is appended automatically by the rendering layer. Do not include it in the article body.
